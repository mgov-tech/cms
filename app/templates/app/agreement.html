Resumo: This is an MIT research project to compare how different text visualizations help researchers understand conversations in online media. We will not track who you are, and any results publications will refer to results in aggregate.

You have been asked to participate in a research study conducted by Ethan Zuckerman, Rahul Bhargava (M.S.), and Rebekah Bell from the MIT Media Lab at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (M.I.T.). The purpose of this study is to compare the performance of different text visualizations used in the Media Cloud suite of web-based tools for analyzing online media. You were selected as a possible participant in this study because of your pre-existing interest in Media Cloud, visualization, or web-based research tools. You should read the information below, and ask questions about anything you do not understand, before deciding whether or not to participate.

  • This survey is voluntary. You have the right not to answer any question, and to stop the survey at any time or for any reason. We expect that the survey will take 15 to 30 minutes.
  • We do not foresee any potential risks of this research study.
  • You could walk away from this study with a more critical mindset when using text visualizations.
  • You will not be compensated for taking this survey.

This project will be completed by June 1, 2018.

Any information that is obtained in connection with this study and that can be identified with you will remain confidential and will be disclosed only with your permission or as required by law. In addition, your information may be reviewed by authorized MIT representatives to ensure compliance with MIT policies and procedures.

If you have any questions or concerns about the research, please feel free to contact Ethan Zuckerman at 617-253-3962 or user-study@mediacloud.org.

If you feel you have been treated unfairly, or you have questions regarding your rights as a research subject, you may contact the Chairman of the Committee on the Use of Humans as Experimental Subjects, M.I.T., Room E25-143b, 77 Massachusetts Ave, Cambridge, MA 02139, phone 1-617-253-6787.
